# 🏥 Royal Hospital — Cybersecurity Awareness Campaigns

> Usable Cybersecurity | Academic Coursework | Keele University

Two security awareness campaigns designed for Royal Hospital, addressing 
evolving cyber threats through targeted, behaviourally-grounded training 
artefacts. Each campaign covers a different user group and artefact format.

---

## 📁 Campaign 1 — Nursing Staff
**Artefact:** Branching Scenario E-Learning Module (HTML5)  
**Threats covered:** Phishing, credential sharing, BYOD, authentication 
fatigue, ransomware response  
**Frameworks:** ISO 27001:2022 · NIST SP 800-50 · NHS DSPT v6.0 · UK GDPR  
**[▶ View Artefact](campaign-1-nurses/artefact/RH_CyberSafe_Nursing_Elearning.html)**

---

## 📁 Campaign 2 — Patients  
**Artefact:** Phishing Simulation Storyboard (HTML5)  
**Threats covered:** Email phishing, SMS smishing, voice phishing (vishing)  
**Frameworks:** NCSC Guidance · UK GDPR · NHS DSPT v6.0 · ISO 27001:2022  
**[▶ View Artefact](campaign-2-patients/artefact/RH_Patient_Phishing_Storyboard.html)**

---

## 🔬 Key Concepts Applied
- COM-B Model of Behaviour Change
- Kirkpatrick Four-Level Evaluation Framework
- Cialdini's Influence Principles (social engineering analysis)
- Kolb's Experiential Learning Cycle
- Beautement et al.'s Compliance Budget Theory
- Situated Learning Theory (Lave & Wenger)

## 📚 Academic Alignment
All campaigns are referenced in IEEE format and aligned with:
NHS Data Security and Protection Toolkit (DSPT) v6.0,
ISO/IEC 27001:2022, NIST SP 800-50, and NCSC guidance.
